Motion Capture

Motion capture or mo-cap, is the name for technology that records the movement of people or objects. The motion is captured by the tech and the data is transferred to a computer program to enable photorealism in a virtual environment. Mo-cap has become increasingly popular in recent years, as a method of imbuing computer-generated 3D characters with the natural and often subtle movements/mannerisms of human actors. Motion capture sessions involve the movements of actors many times per second. Only the movements are recorded - not their real visual appearance. The animation data is then mapped to a 3D model which is enabled to perform the actions that were captured.

Low Lantency

Motion capture can produce almost real-time results, which can reduce the cost of keyframe-based animation.

Less Work

The amount of work required doesn't increase as much with the length or complexity of a performance as it does with traditional techniques.

Realistic interactions

Motion capture can easily recreate complex movements and physical interactions, such as weight and force exchange.

Large amounts of data

Motion capture can produce a lot of animation data in a short amount of time, which can help meet deadlines and be cost effective.

More creative performances

Directors can create more unusual performances or personalities for characters and actors using motion capture.
